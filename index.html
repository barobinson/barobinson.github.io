I am now a postdoc in the group of Mathias Beiglböck, as part of the Mathematical Finance and Probability Theory Group at the University of Vienna.

You can contact me at ben.robinson 'at' univie.ac.at, or see my full contact details below.

 

I received my PhD from the University of Bath in October 2020. My thesis was titled Stochastic control problems for multidimensional martingales and supervised by Alexander Cox.

I was a member of the EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training in Statistical Applied Maths at Bath, or SAMBa for short, and Prob-L@b, the probability group at Bath.

From May to October 2020 I was also a Research Associate in the Institute for Mathematical Innovation at the University of Bath.

Here is my full CV.

My Research

My research is in stochastic control problems for martingales. Specifically, I am interested in:

  • Problems motivated by robust finance and martingale optimal transport;
  • Stochastic control problems related to Monge-Ampère equations;
  • Weak and strong formulations of stochastic control problems;
  • Analysis of viscosity solutions to elliptic PDEs.
